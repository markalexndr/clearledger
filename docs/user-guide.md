# User Guide

This guide explains how to use ClearLedger to create a simple financial snapshot.

ClearLedger is designed to provide a quick overview of your financial position by comparing income, expenses, debts and assets.

No account is required and all data remains stored locally in your browser.

---

# Overview

ClearLedger works by asking for four numbers:

- Monthly income
- Monthly expenses
- Total debts
- Total assets

From these values the application calculates:

- Monthly balance
- Net worth

These values appear instantly in the Summary section.

---

# Getting Started

To begin using ClearLedger:

1. Open the application in your browser.
2. Enter values into the four financial fields.
3. The summary section will automatically update.

No login or installation is required.

---

# Entering Financial Information

## Income

Enter your total **monthly income**.

This may include:

- Salary
- Pension income
- Freelance income
- Other regular income sources

Enter the total amount before deductions if you want a simple overview.

---

## Expenses

Enter your **total monthly expenses**.

Typical expenses may include:

- Rent or mortgage
- Food
- Utilities
- Transport
- Subscriptions
- Other regular spending

The goal is to estimate your total monthly outgoings.

---

## Debts

Enter the **total amount of outstanding debts**.

This might include:

- Credit cards
- Loans
- Overdraft balances
- Other financial liabilities

Only enter the remaining balance, not the monthly payment.

---

## Assets

Enter the **total value of assets you own**.

Examples include:

- Savings accounts
- Investments
- Cash reserves

Assets represent financial resources you currently hold.

---

# Understanding the Summary

The Summary section shows two values.

## Monthly Balance

Monthly Balance represents the difference between income and expenses.

Income – Expenses

If the result is positive, you have a monthly surplus.

If the result is negative, your spending exceeds your income.

---

## Net Worth

Net Worth represents the difference between assets and debts.

Assets – Debts

A positive value indicates assets exceed debts.

A negative value indicates liabilities exceed assets.

---

# Automatic Updates

ClearLedger automatically recalculates results whenever values are entered or updated.

You do not need to press the Calculate button, although it remains available if you prefer to use it.

---

# Data Storage

All information entered into ClearLedger is stored locally in your browser.

This allows values to remain available when the page is refreshed.

No data is transmitted to any server.

---

# Resetting the Tool

To clear all stored values:

1. Click the **Reset** button.
2. All fields will be cleared.
3. Stored browser data will be removed.

This restores the application to its original state.

---

# Privacy Notes

ClearLedger operates entirely within your browser.

It does not:

- collect personal information
- send data to external services
- require user accounts

All calculations occur locally on your device.
